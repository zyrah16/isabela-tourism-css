Aguinaldo Shrine:
A Turning Point in History

History

The Aguinaldo Shrine in Palanan commemorates the historic capture of General Emilio Aguinaldo, the first President of the Philippines, by American forces on March 23, 1901, marking a turning point in the Philippine Revolution’s final chapter. The site honors Filipino resilience and patriotism.

Activities

Visitors usually observe historical markers, learn about the events of the Philippine Revolution, and take photos.

Best Time to Visit

Morning to early afternoon for comfortable weather and clearer views of the shrine and any plaques.

How to Get There

Palanan is relatively remote; visitors typically travel by land via Ilagan or through regional transport services to reach the town center, then take local transport to the shrine.

Tips

Wear comfortable walking shoes and bring water; heritage sites may have uneven paths or minimal facilities.

A tribute to a pivotal moment in Philippine history, reflecting courage and the struggle for independence.
