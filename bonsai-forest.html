Bonsai Forest:
A Forest Like No Other

About

Bonsai Forest in Dinapigue features a vast area of naturally miniature trees — ranging from small to several feet tall — growing wildly amidst the forest. The forest is a striking ecosystem that stretches across thousands of hectares of the Sierra Madre foothills.

Activities

Hiking through forest trails, nature photography, appreciating flora diversity.

Best Time to Visit

Dry season (November–April) for easier trekking and clearer views.

How to Get There

Dinapigue is remote — typically reached by road from Santiago or Cauayan, then by local transport or guide into the forest. Check permits with local tourism offices.

Tips

Bring water and supplies, wear sturdy shoes, and hire a guide — the area is vast and can be confusing without local knowledge.
