Bambanti Festival

About

The Bambanti Festival is Isabela’s flagship cultural‑agricultural celebration held every fourth week of January at the provincial capital area in City of Ilagan. “Bambanti” means scarecrow in Ilocano — a traditional guardian of rice and corn fields — and it symbolizes the industry, resilience, and gratitude of the province’s farmers. Originally launched in 1997 and revived in 2011, the festival brings together all 34 municipalities and three cities to thank the land for its bounty and to highlight local products, creativity, and community pride.

Highlights

The festival features street dance competitions, giant scarecrow installations, trade fairs showcasing local products and food, beauty pageants (like Miss Queen Isabela), concerts, exhibits, competitions like “Makan Ken Mainum,” and cultural performances. Vibrant costumes, music, and arts spotlight local heritage and agricultural success.

Best Time to Visit

Every January during the week of celebration — cooler weather and peak festival activities make this the ideal time to go.

Tips

Plan ahead for accommodations as hotels fill up during festival week. Wear comfortable shoes, bring water, and try local delicacies from food booths.

Bambanti Festival is a vibrant expression of Isabeleño culture and gratitude — a perfect blend of tradition, thanksgiving, and high‑spirited celebration of agriculture and community life.
