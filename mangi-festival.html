Mangi Festival

About

The Mangi/Mammangi Festival is celebrated every February 22–25 in Tumauini to honor corn (mangi in Ibanag), a staple crop that defines the town’s agricultural identity. As a major corn producer, Tumauini uses this festival to highlight cultural pride, community creativity, and the importance of corn in local life.

Highlights

Street dancing shows, parades, local product exhibits, cultural presentations, and fiestas coincide with the town’s patronal feast day. Corn‑inspired costumes, music, and dances are central to the celebration.

Best Time to Visit

Late February, during the scheduled festival days when the community gathers in full celebration.

Tips

Wear cool clothes and sun protection — many events are outdoor and can be warm.

Mangi Festival celebrates Tumauini’s agricultural roots, community spirit, and the hardworking farmers who keep corn at the heart of local life.
