Shrine of Our Lady of the Visitation of Guibang:
A Place of Faith and Devotion

History

The National Shrine of Our Lady of the Visitation of Guibang is a major Catholic pilgrimage site in Barangay Guibang, Gamu, Isabela. It houses the venerated image of Our Lady of the Visitation, a Marian icon that was episcopally crowned in 1973 and elevated to a national shrine in 1986. The current church was enlarged and solemnly dedicated in February 2023, making it a peaceful and spiritually significant landmark along the Maharlika Highway.

Activities

Visitors can attend Mass, offer prayers, light candles, join processions or simply reflect in the serene interior. Many pilgrims visit during the feast on July 2 each year.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning or late afternoon for cooler weather and fewer crowds. If you want to witness the feast and procession, plan for July 2.

How to Get There

From Ilagan City or Cauayan City, take a bus or jeepney to Gamu. The shrine is located along the Maharlika Highway in Brgy. Guibang and is easily accessible by road.

Tips

Dress respectfully for a religious site. Bring a fan or pamaypay for comfort inside the church, and be courteous during services.

A must‑visit for those seeking spiritual reflection and a glimpse into northern Luzon’s rich Catholic devotion.
