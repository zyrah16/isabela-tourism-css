Ilagan Japanese Tunnel:
Echoes of the Past

History

Located in Barangay Santo Tomas, Ilagan City, the Ilagan Japanese Tunnel is a World War II‑era tunnel built by Japanese forces as a military shelter and base. Measuring about 40 meters long and over three meters in width and height, it offers a glimpse into wartime engineering and history.

Activities

Explore the tunnel corridors, imagine wartime strategies, and view remnants of its structure.

Best Time to Visit

Morning to early afternoon when lighting and visibility are better for exploring the tunnel.

How to Get There

From Ilagan City center, take local transport to Barangay Santo Tomas and follow posted signs to the tunnel site.

Tips

Bring a flashlight or use your phone’s light, wear comfortable shoes, and be cautious on uneven ground.

Perfect for history enthusiasts who want to step back into the WWII era in northern Luzon.
