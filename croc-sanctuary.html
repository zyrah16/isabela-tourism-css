Crocodile Sanctuary
in San Marino

About

The sanctuary protects the critically endangered Philippine crocodile (Crocodylus mindorensis) in its natural habitat within rivers and wetlands of San Mariano. It’s a community‑based conservation area supported by Mabuwaya Foundation and local authorities.

Activities

Guided tours to see crocodiles in rearing pens, educational talks on conservation, wildlife watching.

Best Time to Visit

Dry season (December–May) for safer trekking conditions. During early morning or late afternoon for the best experience.

How to Get There

San Mariano is accessible by land from Ilagan or Cauayan City. Local tours to the sanctuary and rearing stations can be arranged through municipal tourism offices or conservation groups.

Tips

Coordinate with guides — the crocodiles live in protected wild areas, so safety and respect for habitat is key.

The Crocodile Sanctuary in San Mariano offers a rare chance to witness conservation in action—where protecting wildlife and preserving nature go hand in hand.
