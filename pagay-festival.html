Pagay Festival

About

The Pagay Festival: Balitok ti Alicia is celebrated every September 28 in Alicia, Isabela, honoring the town’s founding anniversary and its primary agricultural product — pagay (rice). It showcases how rice helped shape Alicia’s identity and history, bringing people together through cultural pride and agricultural appreciation.

Highlights

This festival includes lively street dance showdowns, decorated floats with rice themes, parades featuring carabaos and harvest motifs, music and singing contests, exhibits, and cultural performances. It’s deeply rooted in celebrating rice and art.

Best Time to Visit

Late September during the festival dates when the town is vibrant with dance, music, and community displays.

Tips

Bring a camera for the street dances and parades. Respect local traffic controls as roads may close during performances.

Pagay Festival reflects both cultural heritage and agricultural pride — a joyous celebration of rice, community heritage, and Alicia’s identity.
