Isabela Museum and Library:
Where History Lives On

History

Located at Arranz Street, Barangay Osmeña, City of Ilagan, the Isabela Museum and Library occupies the former Isabela Provincial Capitol Building (1950–1991). The facility preserves cultural artifacts, historical exhibits, and a large collection of books and periodicals, including donated collections reaching tens of thousands of titles. It serves as a custodian of the province’s history and cultural heritage.

Activities

Explore permanent and rotating exhibits on local history, art, and culture; read in the library; and discover heritage pieces and stories of Isabela’s past.

Best Time to Visit

Visit during opening hours (usually weekday mornings/afternoons) to avoid crowds and make the most of your visit.

How to Get There

The museum is in the heart of Ilagan City. From the city center, you can take a tricycle or jeepney and alight near Osmeña Street.

Tips

Bring a notebook or camera for notes and photos. Check ahead for any special exhibits or keynote events.

This venue is ideal for history buffs, students, and anyone curious about Isabela’s cultural roots.
